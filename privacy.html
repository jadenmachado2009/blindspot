Privacy Policy

Last updated: 21 July 2026

The short version: Blindspot runs entirely in your browser. Your assessment answers and bias profile stay on your device and are never sent to us. We use a privacy-friendly, cookie-free analytics tool to count visits — nothing more.

1. Data you enter into the Tool

Your answers, bias profile, and any decision you check are processed and stored locally in your own browser (in your browser's local storage). This data is never transmitted to us or to any server. We cannot see it. Clearing your browser data will erase it.

2. Analytics

To understand how many people visit the site, we use GoatCounter, a privacy-focused analytics service. GoatCounter:

  • does not use cookies and does not track you across sites;
  • does not collect personal information such as your name or email;
  • records only aggregate, non-identifying data — the page visited, the referring site, your approximate country, and general browser/device type;
  • does not store your IP address; it is used transiently only to derive country and a daily, non-reversible visitor count, then discarded.

This data cannot reasonably be used to identify you and is used solely to measure traffic.

3. What we do not do

We do not sell, rent, or share your data. We do not run advertising or ad-tracking. We do not create user accounts or profiles about you.

4. Hosting

The site is hosted on GitHub Pages. Like any web host, GitHub may automatically log standard technical request data (such as IP address and browser type) for security and operational purposes, governed by GitHub's privacy statement.

5. Children

Blindspot is not directed at children and is intended for users aged 16 and over. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

6. Your rights

Because we do not hold personal data about you, there is nothing for us to access, correct, or delete on your behalf. You control the data in your browser and can clear it at any time.

7. Changes

We may update this policy by posting a revised version on this page, with a new "last updated" date.
